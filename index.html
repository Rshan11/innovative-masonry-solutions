Paint vs. Stain: Know the Difference

Most people think paint is the only option. It's not—and here's why that matters for your wallet.

Paint

  • Sits on top of the brick (a coating)
  • Peels, chips, and fades in 5-7 years
  • Traps moisture, causes spalling
  • Requires repainting every few years
  • Looks like painted brick (not natural)

Total cost over 20 years:

$15,000 - $25,000+

(Initial + 3-4 repaints)

Stain

  • Penetrates and bonds with the brick
  • Won't peel, chip, or fade
  • Lets brick breathe naturally
  • Zero maintenance required
  • Looks like natural brick (because it is)

Total cost over 20 years:

$6,000 - $15,000

(One time, lifetime warranty)

Staining costs more upfront but pays for itself—and you never think about it again.

Common Questions

Why does staining cost more than paint?

The materials are more expensive (professional-grade mineral stains vs. latex paint), the application process is more technical, and it requires 25+ years of masonry expertise to do correctly. But you only pay once—ever.

How long does it last?

Forever. Stain becomes part of the brick itself. It doesn't sit on top like paint, so there's nothing to peel, chip, or fade. We back it with a lifetime warranty from Masonry Cosmetics.

Can you match any color?

We can go darker or change the tone (warmer, cooler, etc.). Going dramatically lighter is possible but may require multiple applications. We'll do a test area first so you see exactly what you're getting.

What about previously painted brick?

If your brick has been painted, we may need to strip the paint first before staining. This adds cost and complexity. We'll assess your specific situation during the consultation.

How long does the process take?

Most projects take 1-3 days depending on size. A fireplace is typically done in a day. Full home exteriors may take 2-3 days. The stain dries quickly and there's minimal disruption.
